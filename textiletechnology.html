Textile Technology

Textile Technology focuses on the study of fibers, yarns, and fabrics, along with the processes involved in their production, finishing, and applications. This field combines engineering principles with artistic design to create and improve textiles used in fashion, home furnishings, and industrial applications.

Key Subjects

-

Fiber Science

Study of natural and synthetic fibers and their properties. -

Yarn Manufacturing

: Techniques for converting fibers into yarns. -

Fabric Manufacturing

: Processes involved in weaving, knitting, and non-woven fabric production. -

Textile Finishing

: Techniques to enhance fabric properties such as durability, color, and texture. -

Textile Testing and Quality Control

: Methods to evaluate fabric performance and quality. -

Fashion Technology

: Basics of garment design, pattern making, and production techniques.

Practical Components

-

Projects

: Development of textile products or processes, often in teams. -

Internships

Skills Acquired

-

Technical Skills

: Proficiency in textile manufacturing processes and quality assessment. -

Analytical Skills

: Ability to analyze fabric properties and production methods. -

Team Collaboration

: Experience working in teams on textile projects. -

Communication Skills

Career Prospects

Graduates can pursue various roles, such as:

- Textile Engineer - Quality Control Inspector - Production Manager - Fashion Designer - Research and Development Specialist

Higher Education Options

After completing the diploma, students may choose to:

- Pursue a Bachelor's degree in Textile Engineering or Fashion Technology. - Enroll in specialized certifications in areas like textile design, garment technology, or quality management.
