Home Science

Home Science is an interdisciplinary field that focuses on the management of home and family life. It integrates knowledge from various domains such as nutrition, textiles, human development, and household management, aiming to enhance the quality of life in communities.

Key Subjects

-

Fundamentals of Nutrition

Basics of human nutrition and dietary planning. -

Textile Science

: Study of fabrics, their properties, and applications in clothing. -

Child Development

: Understanding the physical, emotional, and social growth of children. -

Family Resource Management

: Techniques for managing household resources effectively. -

Home Management

: Skills for organizing and managing household tasks and environments. -

Health and Hygiene

: Importance of sanitation, health practices, and disease prevention.

Practical Components

-

Laboratories

: Hands-on experience in nutrition, textiles, and child development. -

Projects

: Development of community-based programs or research projects related to home science. -

Internships

: Industry exposure through internships or practical training in relevant fields.

Skills Acquired

-

Technical Skills

: Proficiency in food preparation, textile design, and child care practices. -

Communication Skills

Graduates can pursue various roles, such as:

- Nutritionist - Child Care Specialist - Home Economics Teacher - Community Development Worker - Textile Designer

Higher Education Options

After completing the diploma, students may choose to:
