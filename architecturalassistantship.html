Architectural Assistantship Engineering

Architectural Assistantship Engineering focuses on the principles of architecture and construction management, preparing students to assist architects and engineers in the design, planning, and execution of building projects. This field combines creativity with technical knowledge to support various architectural processes.

Key Subjects

Architectural Design

Basics of design principles and practices in architecture. -

Construction Technology

Environmental Design

Projects

Internships

Technical Skills

Analytical Skills

Team Collaboration

Communication Skills

Career Prospects

Graduates can pursue various roles, such as:

- Architectural Assistant - Draftsman - Construction Coordinator - Site Supervisor - Building Inspector

Higher Education Options

After completing the diploma, students may choose to:

- Pursue a Bachelor's degree in Architecture or Civil Engineering. - Enroll in specialized certifications in areas like landscape architecture, urban planning, or sustainable design.
