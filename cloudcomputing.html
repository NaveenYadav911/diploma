Cloud Computing

Cloud Computing focuses on the delivery of computing services over the internet, including storage, processing, and software. This field encompasses various models and technologies, preparing students for careers in IT infrastructure, service management, and cloud solutions.

Course Structure:

1. Duration

Usually a 3-year program.

2. Semester System

Divided into six semesters, each covering specific subjects and practical training.

Key Subjects:

Introduction to Cloud Computing

Overview of cloud computing concepts, models, and architectures.

Virtualization Technologies

Techniques for creating virtual instances of hardware and software resources.

Cloud Service Models

Study of Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS), Platform as a Service (PaaS), and Software as a Service (SaaS).

Cloud Security and Compliance

Principles of securing cloud environments and ensuring regulatory compliance.

Data Management in the Cloud

Techniques for managing and storing data in cloud platforms.

DevOps and Continuous Integration

Practices for integrating development and operations in cloud-based environments.

Practical Components:

Laboratories

Hands-on experience with cloud platforms, tools, and service deployments.

Projects

Development of cloud applications or solutions, often in teams.

Internships

Industry exposure through internships or live projects, enhancing employability.

Skills Acquired:

Technical Skills

Proficiency in cloud platforms (AWS, Azure, Google Cloud), virtualization tools, and scripting.

Analytical Skills

Ability to analyze system requirements and optimize cloud solutions.

Team Collaboration

Experience working in teams on cloud computing projects.

Communication Skills

Skills in documenting and presenting technical information effectively.

Career Prospects:

Graduates can pursue various roles, such as:

Cloud Solutions Architect

Cloud Engineer

Systems Administrator

DevOps Engineer

Cloud Consultant

Higher Education Options:

After completing the diploma, students may choose to:

Pursue a Bachelor's degree in Cloud Computing, Information Technology, or related fields.

Enroll in specialized certifications in areas like cloud architecture, security, or DevOps.

Admission Criteria:

Eligibility:

Generally, completion of 10th or 12th grade, depending on the polytechnic.

Entrance Exams:

Some institutions may require entrance exams for admission.

Industry Partnerships:

Many polytechnics have collaborations with cloud service providers and tech companies, enhancing learning opportunities through workshops, guest lectures, and job placements.
