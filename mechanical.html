Mechanical Engineering

Mechanical Engineering focuses on the design, analysis, and manufacturing of mechanical systems. It covers various topics such as thermodynamics, fluid mechanics, and materials science. Graduates can pursue careers in automotive design, aerospace engineering, and manufacturing, among others.

Here’s a structured overview of the Mechanical Engineering branch in polytechnic:

Course Structure:

1. Duration

Usually a 3-year program.

2. Semester System

Divided into six semesters, each covering specific subjects and practical training.

Key Subjects

-

Engineering Mechanics

: Fundamentals of forces, moments, and equilibrium in mechanical systems. -

Thermodynamics

: Study of energy transfer, heat engines, and thermodynamic cycles. -

Fluid Mechanics

: Understanding the behavior of fluids in motion and at rest. -

Material Science

: Properties of materials and their applications in engineering. -

Machine Design

: Principles of designing mechanical components and systems. -

Manufacturing Processes

: Techniques for shaping and assembling materials into products.

Practical Components

-

Laboratories

: Hands-on experience in mechanics, thermodynamics, and manufacturing processes. -

Projects

: Development of mechanical systems or devices, often in teams. -

Internships

: Industry exposure through internships or live projects, enhancing employability.

Skills Acquired

-

Technical Skills

: Proficiency in mechanical design, analysis, and manufacturing techniques. -

Analytical Skills

: Ability to solve complex engineering problems and conduct experiments. -

Team Collaboration

: Experience working in teams on engineering projects. -

Communication Skills

: Skills in documenting and presenting technical information effectively.

Career Prospects

Graduates can pursue various roles, such as:

- Mechanical Engineer - Design Engineer - Manufacturing Engineer - Quality Control Engineer - Maintenance Engineer

Higher Education Options

After completing the diploma, students may choose to:

- Pursue a Bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering or related fields. - Enroll in specialized certifications in areas like CAD, robotics, or advanced manufacturing.

Admission Criteria

Eligibility
